Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 10, 2026 | Last Updated: May 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Search Box Strategies ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and protects information when you use DentalAppeal (the "Service"), our AI-powered dental insurance appeal platform.

⚠️ HIPAA COMPLIANCE NOTICE: DentalAppeal is a HIPAA-compliant platform designed for dental practices. We serve as a Business Associate under HIPAA and protect all Protected Health Information (PHI) in accordance with federal regulations. A separate Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is required before processing PHI.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Practice Information: Practice name, address, phone, fax, NPI number, tax ID, and provider credentials
  • User Account Information: Name, email address, password, and role within the practice
  • Patient Information (PHI): Patient names, dates of birth, insurance information, procedure codes, clinical notes, denial reasons, and supporting documentation (X-rays, EOBs, clinical notes)
  • Billing Information: Payment method details (processed by Stripe, not stored by us)

1.2 Information Automatically Collected

  • Usage Data: Log files, IP addresses, browser type, device information, and pages visited
  • Audit Logs: Timestamps of PHI access, modifications, and exports
  • Performance Data: Appeal generation times, success rates, and feature usage

1.3 Information from AI Processing

  • Appeal Letters: Generated letters are stored for your records and can be exported at any time
  • Processing Metadata: AI model versions, token usage, and generation timestamps

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Service Delivery: To generate appeal letters, track claim status, and provide the Service
  • AI Processing: To generate clinical justifications and appeal letters using Claude AI (Anthropic)
  • Practice Management: To aggregate claims data, track success rates, and provide analytics
  • Communication: To send invoices, payment receipts, and important Service updates
  • Compliance: To maintain audit logs, investigate security incidents, and comply with legal obligations
  • Service Improvement: To improve AI models and features using de-identified data

3. HIPAA Compliance and Protected Health Information

3.1 Business Associate Role

DentalAppeal acts as a Business Associate under HIPAA. We:

  • Only use PHI as permitted by our BAA and these Terms
  • Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards for PHI
  • Report any security incidents or breaches promptly
  • Make PHI available for patient access requests
  • Maintain PHI in accordance with HIPAA retention requirements

3.2 Security Measures

We protect PHI through multiple security layers:

  • Encryption: PHI is encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Access Controls: Role-based permissions, unique user IDs, and automatic session timeout
  • Audit Controls: Comprehensive logs of PHI access, modifications, and exports
  • Secure Infrastructure: Hosted on HIPAA-compliant Hostinger infrastructure
  • AI Processing: All AI API calls occur over encrypted channels with Anthropic's HIPAA-compliant service

3.3 Minimum Necessary Standard

We adhere to the HIPAA Minimum Necessary Standard, limiting PHI access to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis.

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure

4.1 Third-Party Service Providers

We share data with the following providers solely to deliver the Service. All providers sign Business Associate Agreements where required:

  • Anthropic (Claude AI): Processes clinical data to generate appeal letters (HIPAA-compliant configuration)
  • Supabase: Hosts encrypted database with PHI
  • Stripe: Processes subscription payments (no PHI shared)
  • Hostinger: Hosts application infrastructure (BAA in place)

4.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or government regulation, or to protect the rights or safety of any person.

4.3 Business Transfers

If the Company is acquired or merges with another entity, user data may be transferred. Notice will be provided before any such transfer.

4.4 No Unauthorized Marketing

We do not sell PHI or use PHI for marketing purposes without explicit authorization. De-identified data may be used for analytics and Service improvement.

5. Data Retention

  • PHI: Retained for 7 years from the last date of Service as required by HIPAA and state dental records laws
  • User Account Data: Retained while account is active, plus 30 days after termination for export
  • Audit Logs: Retained for 6 years for compliance and security investigations
  • De-identified Data: May be retained indefinitely for research and Service improvement

6. User Rights

Customer (the dental practice) has the following rights regarding Client Data:

  • Access: Request a copy of all PHI stored in the Service
  • Correction: Request corrections to inaccurate PHI
  • Export: Export all Client Data in machine-readable format
  • Deletion: Request deletion of PHI after the required retention period
  • Accounting of Disclosures: Request a record of PHI disclosures

To exercise these rights, contact privacy@dentalappeal.claims.

7. International Data Transfers

All data is stored on servers located in the United States. If you access the Service from outside the US, you consent to the transfer of your information to the US.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

9. Business Associate Agreement (BAA)

Before processing any PHI, dental practices must execute a separate Business Associate Agreement. The BAA supplements this Privacy Policy and governs the handling of PHI under HIPAA.

To request a BAA, contact legal@dentalappeal.claims.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Material changes will be notified via email or through the Service. The effective date at the top indicates when the policy was last revised.

11. Security Incident Response

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, we will:

  • Notify affected customers without unreasonable delay
  • Investigate the incident and take corrective action
  • Assist customers with breach notification obligations
  • Report to the Office for Civil Rights as required by law

12. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected
  • Request deletion of personal information
  • Opt out of the sale of personal information (we do not sell personal information)
  • Non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights
